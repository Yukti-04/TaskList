Career timeline of Pusarla Venkata Sindhu

  • 1995:- PV Sindhu was born on 5th July 1995

  • 2009:- Sindhu bronze medalist at the 2009 Sub-Junior Asian Badminton Championships held in Colombo

  • 2010 -
    • Won silver medal at Iran Fajr International Badminton Challenge in the singles category.
    • She reached the quarterfinals of the 2010 BWF World Junior Championships that was held in Mexico

  • 2011:- In 2011, she won the Maldives International Challenge in June defeating compatriot P.C. Thulasi

  • 2012:- Won the Asian Junior Championships, becoming India's first-ever Asian Junior Champion

  • 2013:-
    • She reached her career-best ranking of 15.
    • She won the Malaysian Grand Prix Gold title.
    • Was awarded the Arjuna Award by the Government of India.

  • 2014:-
    • She claimed her first medal at the Asian Championships, a bronze.
    • She reached the semi-final stage of the 2014 Commonwealth Games in the women's singles competition, which she lost narrowly to eventual gold medalist Michelle Li of Canada. She later won against Malaysian Tee Jing Yi to claim the bronze medal.

  • 2015:- She suffered a stress fracture in 2015 that kept her from playing for nearly six months, yet managed to qualify for the 2016 Rio Olympics.

  • 2016:- She clinched the silver medal, creating history as India's youngest individual Olympic medallist and the first Indian woman to bag an Olympic silver medal.This was only the second instance of a podium finish at the Olympics by any Indian badminton player.

  • 2017:-
    • Sindhu won the Syed Modi International by beating Gregoria Mariska Tunjung of Indonesia in the final.
    • She achieved a career-high world ranking of number 2.
    • Sindhu defeated Okuhara in the final of the 2017 Korea Open Super Series becoming the first Indian to win the Korea Open.

  • 2018:-
    • She competed at the 2018 Commonwealth Games in Gold Coast, winning a gold medal in the mixed team event and a silver medal in the singles event.
    • She won a historic first silver medal for India in badminton at 2018 Asian Games.
    • Become the only shuttler from India to claim the title at the year-end finale at 2018 BWF World Tour Finals.

  • 2019:-
    • She had ended her deal with Yonex and signed a mega-deal with Li-Ning for 4 years worth nearly ₹500 million
    • She became the first Indian to win gold at the World Championships

  • 2020:-
    • She was named the BBC Indian Sportswoman of Year on 8 March 2020
    • In April, she was elected as one of the ambassadors of the BWF Committee's campaign – "I am Badminton" to promote clean and fair play in the sport.

  • 2021:-
    • She was elected as one of the two ambassadors from badminton in the International Olympic Committee's campaign ‘Believe in Sport’, aimed at preventing competition manipulation in the sport.
    • At Tokto Olympics she clinched the bronze medal, thereby becoming the first Indian woman and only the fourth player in women's singles badminton to claim two medals at two consecutive Olympic games

  • 2022:-
    • Sindhu won the Syed Modi International for the second time.
    • She then won the title at the 2022 Swiss Open.
    • She then won the Singapore Open title.
